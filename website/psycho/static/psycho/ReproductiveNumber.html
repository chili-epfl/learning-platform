Epidemic - Basic Reproductive Number

The basic reproduction rate called Ro(R nought) is used to measure the transmission potential of disease. It is thought of as the number of secondary infections produced by a typical case of an infection in a population that is totally susceptible to get infected. It can therefore be measured by counting the number of secondary cases following the introduction of an infection into a totally susceptible population.
Ro = Number of new cases of infection / Number of initial cases, during an infectious period of a suceptible popoulation.

The basic reproductive rate Ro is also set to be the product of the following three factors:

  • S = The Number of suceptible hosts there are to get infected.
  • L = The duration of infectiousness of an individual. We can think that the more individuals remain infectious the longer they can transmit to others.
    S and L give us the potential number of contact over the course of an infection.
  • Beta = The probability of an infection being transmitted during contact (Transmissibility).

An outbreak is only likely to spread if Ro >1 .
